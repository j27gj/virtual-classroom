Virtual Classroom FAQ




What is Virtual Classroom?

Virtual Classroom is a virtual learning platform that allows instructors to host classes online from the comfort of their home without changing their teaching style. Instructors and students are able to interact and collaborate virtually as they would in a physical classroom. is a virtual learning platform that allows instructors to host classes online from the comfort of their home without changing their teaching style. Why did you create Virtual Classroom? There are so many online class platforms already.

1.We believe teaching online should not be scary. We wanted to enable instructors to teach naturally without having to learn complex user interfaces (e.g. Blackboard Illuminate) or manage multiple different applications simultaneously (chat, whiteboard, PowerPoint sharing, etc.). The focus should be on teaching, not technology.

2.We believe in delivering a high quality online experience that leads to improved learning outcomes. We wanted to bring back the dynamics of a synchronous, live classroom to an online community. On the one hand, video conferencing does not scale and the lag time can be really disconcerting. On the other hand, services like Webex are incredibly limiting in terms of use cases.

3.We believe in improving accessibility and want to make online education available globally. GatherEducation uses much less bandwidth than video services and can scale at a much lower cost than any other collaborative experience.

Is the platform free to use?

Virtual Classroom is currently in private beta, which means that it is free to use. All we ask for is your feedback on how we can make things better!

What do students need to participate in a class?

To participate in a Virtual Classroom, all a student needs is a computer with Internet access and a microphone that is turned on. For specific operating system requirements, see below. To join a class, students simply go to the room link provided by their instructors at the scheduled time.
